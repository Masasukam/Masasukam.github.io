About Me

"Get it done, or let it go"

I am currently pursuing my Master of Engineering in Computer Science at Cornell University, with a graduation date set for December 2024. Previously, I completed my Bachelor of Science Computer Science and Mathematics at University of British Columbia.

At Cornell University, I am primarily focusing on AI/ML, parallel computing, and computer vision. I am utilizing Pytorch/Tensorflow in particular to solve problems related to 3D-Reconstruction, feature detection, and automating tasks, etc. Additionally, I am researching on parallel computing techniques to optimize the performance of computational simulations in the context of large-scale data processing across GPUs, shared memory, and distributed memory systems.

That being said, I have 5-6 years of experience with writing significant amounts of code with experience in API development, Java, C, Python, and many other languages. I thrive on the challenge of maintaining a strong product focus, always striving to deliver the best possible experience for users. Additionally, I enjoy giving demos, as it allows me to showcase the impact of the software I help create.
